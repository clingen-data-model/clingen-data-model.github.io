Criterion

A single rule for evaluating data in an interpretation.

Attributes

Name Type Cardinality Description
description String 0..1
shortDescription String 0..1
defaultStrength CodeableConcept 0..1

Examples

ID description shortDescription defaultStrength

PVS1

null variant (nonsense, frameshift, canonical ±1 or 2 splice sites, initiation codon, single or multiexon deletion) in a gene where LOF is a known mechanism of disease

Null variant in gene with known LOF mechanism

CG-evidence-strength:pvs

PS1

Same amino acid change as a previously established pathogenic variant regardless of nucleotide change

Nucleotide change results in previously reported pathogenic amino acid change

CG-evidence-strength:ps

PS2

De novo (both maternity and paternity confirmed) in a patient with the disease and no family history

Confirmed de novo in individual without family history of condition

CG-evidence-strength:ps

PS3

Well-established in vitro or in vivo functional studies supportive of a damaging effect on the gene or gene product

Well-established functional studies showing damaging effect

CG-evidence-strength:ps

PS4

The prevalence of the variant in affected individuals is significantly increased compared with the prevalence in controls

Prevelance increase in affected vs. control

CG-evidence-strength:ps

PM1

Located in a mutational hot spot and/or critical and well-established functional domain (e.g., active site of an enzyme) without benign variation

Located in hot spot without benign variation

CG-evidence-strength:pm

PM2

Absent from controls (or at extremely low frequency if recessive) (Table 6) in Exome Sequencing Project, 1000 Genomes Project, or Exome Aggregation Consortium

Absent from controls

CG-evidence-strength:pm

PM3

For recessive disorders, detected in trans with a pathogenic variant

Recessive in trans with pathogenic variant

CG-evidence-strength:pm

PM4

Protein length changes as a result of in-frame deletions/insertions in a nonrepeat region or stop-loss variants

Protein length changes or stop-loss

CG-evidence-strength:pm

PM5

Novel missense change at an amino acid residue where a different missense change determined to be pathogenic has been seen before

Novel missense at same amino acid as known pathogenic missense

CG-evidence-strength:pm

PM6

Assumed de novo, but without confirmation of paternity and maternity

Assumed de novo (parental status not confirmed)

CG-evidence-strength:pm

PP1

Cosegregation with disease in multiple affected family members in a gene definitively known to cause the disease

Segregates with disease in affected family members

CG-evidence-strength:pp

PP2

Missense variant in a gene that has a low rate of benign missense variation and in which missense variants are a common mechanism of disease

Missense in condition where pathogenic missense common

CG-evidence-strength:pp

PP3

Multiple lines of computational evidence support a deleterious effect on the gene or gene product (conservation, evolutionary, splicing impact, etc.)

Computational evidence supports deleterious impact

CG-evidence-strength:pp

PP4

Patient’s phenotype or family history is highly specific for a disease with a single genetic etiology

Disease specific phenotype and family history

CG-evidence-strength:pp

PP5

Reputable source recently reports variant as pathogenic, but the evidence is not available to the laboratory to perform an independent evaluation

Reported pathogenic by reputable source without shared data

CG-evidence-strength:pp

BA1

Allele frequency is >5% in Exome Sequencing Project, 1000 Genomes Project, or Exome Aggregation Consortium

Present in population databases at greater than 5%

CG-evidence-strength:ba

BS1

Allele frequency is greater than expected for disorder

MAF too high for disorder

CG-evidence-strength:bs

BS2

Observed in a healthy adult individual for a recessive (homozygous), dominant (heterozygous), or X-linked (hemizygous) disorder, with full penetrance expected at an early age

Control frequency higher than expected for penetrance

CG-evidence-strength:bs

BS3

Well-established in vitro or in vivo functional studies show no damaging effect on protein function or splicing

Well-established functional studies do not show damaging effect

CG-evidence-strength:bs

BS4

Lack of segregation in affected members of a family

Does not segregate with disease

CG-evidence-strength:bs

BP1

Missense variant in a gene for which primarily truncating variants are known to cause disease

Missense variant when truncating is expected mechanism

CG-evidence-strength:bp

BP2

Observed in trans with a pathogenic variant for a fully penetrant dominant gene/disorder or observed in cis with a pathogenic variant in any inheritance pattern

In trans with dominant OR in cis with pathogenic variant

CG-evidence-strength:bp

BP3

In-frame deletions/insertions in a repetitive region without a known function

In-frame indel without known function

CG-evidence-strength:bp

BP4

Multiple lines of computational evidence suggest no impact on gene or gene product (conservation, evolutionary, splicing impact, etc.)

Computational evidence supports benign impact

CG-evidence-strength:bp

BP5

Variant found in a case with an alternate molecular basis for disease

Variant found in case with alternate explanation

CG-evidence-strength:bp

BP6

Reputable source recently reports variant as benign, but the evidence is not available to the laboratory to perform an independent evaluation

Reported benign by reputable source without shared data

CG-evidence-strength:bp

BP7

A synonymous (silent) variant for which splicing prediction algorithms predict no impact to the splice consensus sequence nor the creation of a new splice site AND the nucleotide is not highly conserved

Synonymous variant without predicted splice effect in region with low conservation

CG-evidence-strength:bp
