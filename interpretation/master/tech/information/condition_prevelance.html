ConditionPrevelance

Frequency of a condition in a population

Descended from Information

Scope and Usage

ConditionPrevalence encodes the rate at which a condition occurs within a population. Especially for rare diseases, measurement of prevalence may be difficult, and it may be expressed as an inequality (e.g. less than 1 in 100000). For this reason, prevalence may be expressed as a simple value, an upper or lower bound, or a combination of the above.

Attributes

Name Type Cardinality Description
condition MendelianCondition 0..* Condition for which the prevelance is described
population CodeableConcept 0..1 Population over which the prevelance is defined
minimum float 0..1 Minimum bound on the condition's prevelance
maximum float 0..1 Maximum bound on the condition's prevelance
prevelance float 0..1 Prevelance of the condition in the population
